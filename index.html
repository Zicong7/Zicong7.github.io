Protect Your Online Privacy

With so much personal information and data that can be accessed without permission, protecting personal information has never been more important. Follow the suggestions below and take a few minutes take control your own data!

Password

Don't make your accounts share the same or easy to associate passwords. Change your password regularly. For important accounts, don't let your browser save password for you.

Photo

Facial data has been used a lot. Uploading photos on social media may bring hidden dangers. Set the account private, or just keep photos local.

Payment

Do not save payment methods in your browser or app. Also, do not save bank account information in your phone. Once the user authorizes payment method in Apps, credit card information is at risk of theft.

Wi-Fi

Do not use unsecured public hotspots to avoid exposing information to hackers under public wifi. Generally, cellular provided by operators is relatively secure than those "Free Wi-fi".

Setting

Settings determine how others can search for you and what information you have is open to the public. Most social networking settings make information public and visible, modify these settings to enhance privacy.

Location

Many social networks, such as Facebook, allow you to add your location when posting. These locations will be recorded. Don't share your location unless you have to.
