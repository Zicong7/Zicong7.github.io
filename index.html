Protect Your Online Privacy

With so much personal privacy data can be accessed, protecting personal information online has never been more important. Follow the few suggestions below and take a few minutes to ensure that your online life is sufficiently private.

Password

Don't make many of your accounts share the same or easy to associate passwords. Change your password regularly. For important accounts, don't let your browser save it for you.

Photo

With the widespread use of facial data, putting too many photos on social media will also bring hidden dangers.Set the account private, or just keep photos local.

Payment

Do not save payment methods in your browser or app. Also, do not record bank account information in your phone. In some software, once the user authorizes payment, bank card information is at risk of leaking. Enter manually when paying.

Wi-Fi

Do not use unsecured public hotspots to avoid exposing information to hackers under public wifi. Generally, cellular traffic provided by operators is relatively secure.

Setting

Settings determine how others can search for you and what information you have is open to the public. Most social networking settings make information visible, modify these settings to enhance privacy.

Location

Many social networks, such as Facebook, allow you to add your location when posting. These locations are then saved to the database. Don't share your location unless you have to share your location when it matters.
